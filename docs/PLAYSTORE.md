# Google Play Store Listing
## Comprehensive Summary of Best Practices and Policies

## I. General Store Listing & Policy Compliance

Creating a successful Google Play Store listing requires adherence to both best practices and mandatory policies.

* **Mandatory Requirements:** Completion of the target-audience and content-setting questionnaire is not optional—it is a mandatory step.
* **Policy Resources:** To ensure compliance, the best resource for information on what is and is not allowed is the **Google Play Developer Policy Center**. As part of the app review process, your app title and developer name are evaluated for compliance with Google Play policies.
* **Listing Limits:** In terms of limitations, an app can have up to **50 custom store listings**.
* **Prohibited Content:** Certain content is strictly not allowed, including data comparison of apps or brands (like claiming superiority to a competitor) and language inappropriate for a general audience.
* **Keyword Spam:** Policy violations often occur with excessive keyword stuffing in the description, such as repeating terms like "dresses" or "dogs," which are not permitted.

## II. App Icon and Graphics Best Practices

Graphics should be optimized to tell a consistent brand story.

* **Design Consistency:** The recommended best practices are to use a similar or complementary color theme and style in all your Store listing graphics and to use brand elements and graphic styles that extend your app icon.
* **Technical Specs:** For a feature graphic, the acceptable formats are **JPEG** and **PNG**. Detailed graphic specifications, including size, format, and color depth, can be found on the Main store listing page on the Google Play Console.
* **App Icons:** The final asset should be a **full square** because Google Play dynamically applies rounded corners. Artwork should be placed on a full-bleed background when no distinct shape is needed around the logo to define it. Acceptable banners to include on your app icon are generic, non-promotional terms like "Fun!" or "Pizza!", while promotional text like "Free!", "Sale!", or "New!" is generally non-compliant.
* **Feature Graphics:** Best practices include keeping prominent visuals towards the center, designing the image to scale to various screen sizes, highlighting in-game visuals and characters, and using vibrant colors to build excitement.
* **Image Permissions:** You must obtain and submit written documentation of permission to use certain images, including:
* Photos from a public figure's social-media account.
* Professional images of public figures (singers, actors, politicians).
* Artwork/images from media (comic books, movies).
* College and professional sports team logos.

## III. App Description and Text Best Practices

A compelling description should include who will be most interested in your app or game, what your app or game does, and the unique benefits of using it.

* **The Long Description:** A good description is concise, provides an overview of the most important features and benefits, and contains enough information for a user to make a quick decision.
* **The Short Description:** It is recommended to give users a summary of your app’s noteworthy benefits. Crucially, you **should not** reuse the short description in the long description.
* **App Titles:** Titles such as "Recipe hero" or "Race Road Stars" should be concise and avoid promotional terms, excessive punctuation, or emojis to follow recommended best practices.
* **Developer Names:** Must comply with policy by avoiding promotional or comparative language. For example, "Weather 365 Team" is compliant, whereas names using terms like "#1," "No Ads," or "Free" violate policy.
* **User Testimonials:** It is highly recommended to **avoid user testimonials entirely** in your app description, as Google Play's automated spam filters frequently flag them as deceptive behavior or keyword spam.
* **Tags:** For discoverability, the recommended maximum number of tags that can be added to your store listing is **five**. It is **not** a best practice to change your app's tags every time you change content or functionality.

## IV. Screenshots and Videos

The primary purpose of screenshots is to display core features and content.

* **Screenshot Best Practices:** Compelling screenshots should focus on core features and utilize taglines for key characteristics. These taglines should focus on benefits from the user’s perspective, but they should **not** use direct calls to action like "Download now!".
* **Promo Videos:** For feature videos, the recommended best practice for maximum length is **30 seconds**.
* **Localization:** To provide a localized experience, it is a recommended best practice to add separate feature images, separate screenshots, and separate promotional videos to your store listing for each language you support.

## V. Custom Store Listings and Experiments

* **Custom Store Listings:** You can create up to **50 custom store listings** to target specific countries or user states. The attributes you can customize are the Title, Icon, Descriptions, and Graphic assets. The added benefits of a custom listing include improving your app installs in specific countries and accurately showcasing your app’s key features in those regions.
* **Translation:** Methods include providing your own translations, using a third-party translation service, and letting Google Play provide an automated translation. You also have the option to purchase translations directly from the Google Play Console.
* **A/B Testing (Experiments):** You can run **one** default graphic experiment at once. Experiments can be performed on the App icon, Feature graphics, Screenshots, Promo video, Short description, and Long description. Price and Category are **not** attributes you can experiment on.
* **Localized Experiments:** Recommended when you want to measure which store listing assets (like Spanish-language screenshots) perform best in a specific language. The variants in these experiments are only shown to users who view your app’s store listing in the languages you choose.
* **Experiment Best Practices:** Run tests for **7 days or more** and create at least three alternatives for testing graphics and text. It is a strict policy that you should **not** test two attributes at a time. If one variant performs significantly better after five days, the recommended best practice is to **continue the experiment** to ensure reliable data is collected over the full recommended duration.

## VI. Store Analysis Report

The store analysis report provides a high-level view of your store's performance across different dimensions over time.

* **Key Metrics:** Metrics available on the report include the percentage of store listing visitors that resulted in acquisitions, the number of users who visited your store listing who did not already have your app installed (visitors), and the number of new users who installed the app after visiting the listing (new acquisitions).
* **Dimensions:** Available dimensions include Country/region, Language, Android version, User device, App version, and Carrier.
* **Acquisition Sources:** For tracking installs from sources like Google Search, peer-to-peer, and backup and restore, the **Statistics** report should be used instead.
